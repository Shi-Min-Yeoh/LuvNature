About Tapir

Predators Wildcats, crocodiles

Scientific name Tapiridae
Numbers left Between 3,000 and 4,500
Conservation Status Malayan tapir – Endangered
Mountain tapir – Endangered
Baird’s tapir – Endangered
Lowland tapir – Vulnerable

Fun Fact

They are great swimmers
Great swimmers

Tapirs spend a lot of their time in water. They submerge themselves in water to cool off.They also walk along the bottom of riverbeds, like hippos, searching for plants to graze on

They have poor eyesight
Poor eyesight

Tapirs have small eyes and monocular vision. Their eyes are unable to coordinate with each other to form one cohesive image but they have good olfactory and auditory sensess.

They are very vocal animals
Vocal animals

A variety of different vocalizations are used by tapirs to communicate.They have been observed to produce high-frequency whistles, as well as grunts, to get their messages across

They might be more sociable than we first thought
They might be more sociable

Once solitary, tapirs now exhibit group foraging, suggesting previously unrecognized social behaviors.
